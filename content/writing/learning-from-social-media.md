I save a lot of stuff.

TikToks about building products. YouTube videos on automation. LinkedIn posts from founders who figured something out I haven't yet. I save them like I'm going to come back to them. Like they mean something.

I never do.

The content disappears into whatever app I saved it in. The knowledge doesn't compound. It's just gone.

This is the problem I built Loomi to solve. And also the problem I have most personally.

---

**Why social media is actually where I learn**

I didn't go to a bootcamp. I didn't have a mentor who sat me down and explained how things work. A significant chunk of what I know about building products, about AI, about running a business — came from the internet. From watching people work in public and thinking okay, I could do that.

Social media gets dismissed as shallow. For some things, it is. But it's also where practitioners share what they're figuring out in real time. That's valuable if you know how to use it.

The problem isn't the content. The problem is what happens after you save it.

---

**What actually works**

I've tried a few things. Here's what stuck:

**Save with intention, not just impulse.** Not "this is interesting" but "this changes something about how I think about X." The bar is higher. I save less. I actually remember what I saved.

**If you can't write one sentence about why you saved something, delete it.** This is brutal but it works. If you can't articulate what it changes for you, it wasn't actually useful — it just felt useful in the moment.

**Review before you start something, not on a schedule.** If I'm starting work in a new area, I go back to everything I've saved on that topic first. That's when it's actually relevant. Scheduled reviews never worked for me — context-triggered ones do.

---

**The Loomi angle**

Loomi is the longer answer to this problem. The idea: you feed it content — videos, calls, articles — and it extracts what's actually useful and makes it searchable.

The deeper thing I learned building it: the content isn't the problem. Your relationship with it is. You need something that forces you to extract value before the algorithm shows you the next thing.

I'm still building it. But I feel the problem it's solving every time I scroll.
